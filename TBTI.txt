1.1 About TBTI 

We fully rely on balanced Bible teaching (Apostles‚Äô Doctrine). Our foundation is the whole counsel of God‚ÄĒfaithfully teaching Scripture in its proper context, grounded in Christ-centered, apostolic truth (See Acts 2:42; Eph. 2:20).

We totally depend on the grace of God. Salvation, growth, ministry, and endurance are all by grace alone, not by human effort or religious performance (See 1 Corinthians 15:10; 1Pet. 1:13). 

We emphasize discipleship, not mere conversion. TBTI exists to make disciples who obey Christ‚Äôs commands, mature in faith, and walk in lifelong obedience (See Matthew 28:19‚Äď20; Eph. 4:12-13). 

We uphold the New Covenant life through the Holy Spirit. We teach that true Christian living is only possible through the indwelling and empowering work of the Holy Spirit, not through legalism (See 2 Corinthians 3:6; Gal. 3:3-4). 

We pursue inward transformation before outward ministry. Character, holiness, and a renewed mind are prioritized over gifting, position, or platform (See Romans 12:1‚Äď2; Gal. 6:15). 

We train believers for biblical ministry and sound doctrine. TBTI equips leaders and workers who can rightly divide the Word of truth and serve the Church faithfully (See 2 Timothy 2:15; 3:15-17). 

We maintain simplicity, purity, and faithfulness to Christ. Our goal is not popularity or trend-driven ministry, but faithfulness to Jesus Christ and His Word (See 2 Corinthians 11:3; Phil 4:8-9).

1.5 STATEMENT OF FAITH 

 

The Triune God (Trinity) ‚Äď We believe in the only one and true God (Deut. 6:4; 1 Tim 2:5), eternally existing in three persons‚ÄĒFather, Son, and the Holy Spirit. They are equal in nature, being, power, glory, attributes, and perfection. Matt.3:16-17; 28:19; Jn. 14:26; 16:7-10, 15; 1 Cor. 12:4-6; 2 Cor. 13:14; Eph. 4:4-6; 1 Jn. 5:8. 

 

The Father ‚Äď We believe that God the Father is eternal, perfect, omnipotent, omniscient omnipresent. We believe that the heavenly Father is the source of everything that exists, whether of matter or spirit. With the Son and the Holy Spirit, He made man, male and female, in His image. He is good and all his ways are perfect. In love, he accepts repentant sinners into His family when they believe in His Son, Jesus Christ. (See Isa. 66:1; 40:28; Job 11:7-8; Matt. 19:26; Jer. 32:27; Eph. 1:19-20; Jer. 23:24; Ps. 139:7-8). 

 

The Son ‚Äď We believe that the Lord Jesus Christ is the eternal Word, co-Creator with the Father God in the beginning of creation, the only Begotten Son of God who left His glorious state, was born of a virgin, conceived through the power of the Holy Spirit, was made fully man, displayed God in the flesh, with the purpose of saving man from sin. (See Jn. 1:1-3; Col. 1:15-17; Phil. 2:6-9; Matt. 1:20-23; Lk. 1:28-35; Heb. 2:17-18; Col. 2:9-10; Lk. 1:76-79). 

 

We believe that Jesus was the perfect sacrifice for the forgiveness of man‚Äôs sin when he willingly offered his life to die on the cross of Calvary, that whoever believes in him, repents from sin and turn to God, willingly goes for water baptism, is marked with the promised Holy Spirit, and is born again into the family of God, justified from sin, and has eternal life, now. (See 1 Jn. 2:2; Ro. 5:8; Matt. 20:28; Acts 2:38; 16:31; 8:12-15; 10:43; 15:7-9; 1 Jn. 5:9-13). 

 

We believe that after Jesus died on the cross, he was raised on the third day, ascended into heaven, is now seated at the right hand of God, and will come back for His faithful saints to judge the world, and install His eternal kingdom after he creates a New Heaven and Earth. (See Matt. 28:6-7; Acts 1:3; 9-11; 7:55-56; Jn. 5:27-29; 1 Thess. 4:14-17; Jn. 14:1-3). 

 

The Holy Spirit ‚Äď We believe that the Holy Spirit is the third person of God, co-creator with the Father and Son, who convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and of judgement; and that He is given as a seal of promise to all who believes in Christ and is regenerated; He indwells in the believers as their Advocate, Comforter, teacher and guide to holy and righteous living, empowers all believers to serve God and His church, until they are redeemed on earth and taken into Christ‚Äôs kingdom in heaven. (See 1 Jn. 5:8; Ps. 104:30; Job 33:4; Jn. 16:8; 2 Cor. 1:21-22; Eph. 1:13-14; Jn. 14:16, 26; 15:26; 1Jn. 2:27; Ro. 8:9). 
 

The Bible ‚Äď We believe that the Bible, composed of 66 books of the Old and New Testament, is the written word of God, inerrant and inspired by God in its entirety. It is the Third gift from God for human‚Äôs guide towards total salvation from sin. It is supreme and final authority in life and faith. (See 2 Tim. 3:16-17; 2 Pet1:20-21; Ps. 19:7b; Ro. 15:4; Prov. 30:5; Matt. 22:29; Eph. 5:26; Jn. 15:3; Heb. 4:12). 

 

The Kingdom in Heaven - We believe God has a Heavenly Kingdom with Angelic population in which the problem of sin started when Satan and one third of the angels rebelled against Him. This interruption of His eternal pleasure, joy and glory will be restored through replacing the rebel angels with humans and knowing they too will fall into sin, plan for the redemptive work through His Son. The book of Revelation reveals and confirms the victorious restoration of God‚Äôs Eternal Kingdom. (See Job 1:6; 2:1-2; Isa. 14:12-15; Ezek. 28:12-17; Isa. 46:9-10; Ps. 2:7-8; Heb. 1:5; 1 Pet. 1:18-21; Rev. 5:7-14; 7:9-12; 21:12). 

 

The Celestial Beings (Angels) ‚Äď We believe in Angels or spirit beings. They are the Kingdom of Heaven‚Äôs population revealed as Seraphim, Cherubim, Archangels and Angels. They were perfect at the beginning created ahead of humanity. But because of Satan‚Äôs rebellion in which one third of them followed him, God intended to replace them with His Church-the victorious believers, being helped by Angels. (See Isa. 6:2-6; Ezek. 10:1-20; Jude 1:9; Job 38:4-7; Ps. 148:1-5; Rev. 12:3-4; 5:6-14; 7:9-12; Heb. 1:14). 

 

The Origin and Fall of Man ‚Äď We believe that man is created from the dust in the image and likeness of God, supposed to rule the universe. But because of Adam and Eve‚Äôs disobedience, the human race inherited the fallen, sinful nature and became alienated from God; and the end of an unregenerated man is eternal damnation in hell; and the only way to be saved from sin is to be born again through believing in Jesus, repenting from sin, be baptized with water, and to receive the Holy Spirit as a gift. (See Gen 1:26-30; Ro. 5:12-19; Col. 1:21; Ro. 3:23; 6:23; Acts 4:12; 2:38). 

 

The Salvation of Humanity. We believe that the main problem of humanity is sin. And the remedy is only from God; this He worked by sending His only Begotten Son as a recompence through His death. Since men‚Äôs problem is sin, it can be dealt in three stages. Justification-God saving them from the penalty of sin through believing the Gospel; Sanctification -God saving them from the power of sin by empowering them through His Spirit and Glorification or Redemption when God saves them from the presence of sin. (See Jonah 2:9: Acts 4:12; Matt. 1:21; Jn.3:16; 1 Cor. 1:2; Eph. 5:25-27; Heb. 12: 14; 2 Pet. 3:14; 1 Jn.1-3; Rev. 19:7-9; Eph. 1:3-5; 2 Pet. 1:3-4; Matt. 24:13).    

 

 

The Christian Life ‚Äď We believe that everyone who is truly born again is a new creature in Christ, and through the power of the Holy Spirit the regenerated man will have victory to grow in the image and likeness of Jesus, imitating his life by knowing how to live out Christ‚Äôs commands; and this new life is now lived separated from all worldly and sinful practices, pleasures, and compromise. (See 2 Cor. 5:17; Jn. 3:5; 1:12-13; 1 Pet. 1:23; 2 Pet. 1:3-4; Ro. 8:28-30; Matt. 28:19-20; Acts 2:42; 1 Jn.2:6; 4:17; 5:4; Eph. 4:11-15).  

 

The Church ‚Äď We believe that the members of the New Covenant church are those who were accepted by God by giving them His Spirit. They are the representation and substitute of Jesus Christ being His body- to continue the live and work He commissioned them to do. They are connected, associated to one another through the unity wrought by the Holy Spirit and the knowledge of His word. They are predestined to grow into Christlikeness by knowing, understanding and living the commands of Christ, breaking bread, worshiping, praying together and serving God and the believers through the Holy Spirit‚Äôs gifts in each individual. (See Acts 2:1-4, 38-47; 15:7-9; Eph. 1:13-14; 1 Cor. 12:27; Eph. 4:6-15). 

 

The Water Baptism ‚Äď We believe that water baptism is essential in men‚Äôs response together with faith and repentance as required by God to give His Holy Spirit to the believer (Acts 2:38). Water baptism is a step or process towards genuine regeneration (Jn. 3:5; Tit. 3:5). Water baptism is not purely symbolical it is where; #1. God saves the person (Mark 16:16; 1 Pet. 3:19-22). #2. God forgives the person and gives His Holy Spirit (Acts 2:38). #3. Our sins are cleansed (Acts 22:16). #4. The believer is united with Christ in His death, burial and resurrection (Ro. 6:3-4).  #5 The believer is cloth in Christ (Gal. 3:27). #6. The believer‚Äôs conscience is cleansed (1 Pet. 3:21). #7. The believer enters into covenant with God (Col. 2:11-12).            

 

The Last Supper ‚Äď We believe in the importance of the believers‚Äô observation and participation of the Last Supper. Observe that it is later called ‚ÄúBreaking of Bread‚ÄĚ in the time of the Apostles possibly to match for global practice. Holy communion is commanded by the Lord Jesus Christ to remember his sacrifice on the cross and the believers‚Äô covenant with Him. (See Luke 22:17-20; Jn. 6:53-56; Jn. 13:1 Cor. 11:23-34; Acts 2:42, 46; 20:7-11). 

 

The Enemy ‚Äď We believe in the existence of Satan, the enemy of God and His people. He was formerly a guardian cherub who rebelled against God with the other angels he deceived, who are now demons living in the spiritual realm, but they are doomed for destruction because of Christ‚Äôs victory on the cross, and they will be sent to eternal lake of fire when God judges every creature. Christians are able to resist and overcome Satan and his demons through the power of the name of Jesus and the truth of God‚Äôs word. (See Job 1:6; 2:1-2; Isa. 14:12-15; Ezek. 28:12-17; Rev. 12:3-4, 9; Matt. 25:41; Rev. 20:10). 

 

The Hell and the Lake of Fire ‚Äď We believe that Hell and the Lake of Fire are two different places but are connected and part of each other. Hell, which is also called ‚ÄúHades.‚ÄĚ Hell is compared to the Seashore while the Lake of fire is the lake or sea itself. Hell is now the destiny of the wicked but after the Final Judgment, both the wicked and Hell will be thrown into the Lake of Fire, also called ‚ÄúSecond Death.‚ÄĚ (See Matt. 25:41; Rev. 20:13-14; 21:8). 

 

The Second Coming of Jesus ‚Äď We believe in the Second Coming of Jesus, also mentioned as His return or coming, for the purpose to take His Bride (the Church) with Him. At that time, He will not set His feet on the Earth but form the clouds will send His angels to fetch the believers form the four corners of the world to gather and meet them in the air. The Church with the voice of the Archangel and the trumpet call, all who have fallen asleep in death will be awaken and those who are alive will be changed with their heavenly glorious bodies in the twinkling of an eye. (See Matt. 24:26-31; Mk. 13:21-27; Lk. 21:25-28; 1 Thess. 4:13-17; Jn. 5:28-29; 1 Cor. 15:51-54). 

 

The Wrath of God ‚Äď We believe in the wrath of God that will be poured out on the face of the earth immediately after the rapture of the Church. This is represented by the Seven Trumpets and Seven Golden Bowls, which commence in Revelation chapter 8. During that time, the marked 144,000 Jewish believers in Rev. 7:1-8, who was not caught up with the church will minister at the pour out of God‚Äôs wrath calling on people to repent and call to God. They (144,000) are fetched in Revelation 15:1-4. The Wrath of God will make the Earth‚Äôs population very scarce.   

 

The Millennial Reign of Christ ‚Äď We believe in the Earthly millennial reign of Jesus Christ with His qualified saint (saved). Another coming of Jesus here on Earth is to establish His Kingdom in Jerusalem. From those who were saved He will make a choice to reign with Him. During that time, Satan will be bound and locked in the Abyss until a thousand year is over (Rev. 20:1-6). The Earth‚Äôs population from the wrath of God (after the caught up of the Church) will again grow. Christ‚Äôs Kingdom will be in Jerusalem, that people must go there to worship Him and without worship people will have no rain which very vital for life during that time. (Zech. 14:16-19). 

 

The End or Culmination of the World ‚Äď We believe in the End of the World. Jesus mentioned it (Matt. 24:31). It is the destruction of the Heavens and the Earth by fire (2 Pet. 3:10). It will happen just after the Millennial Reign of Christ when Satan is released from his prison to deceive the Nations to lead them to war against the Government of Christ but fire from Heaven will rain down on Earth which later (Earth) fled from the presence of God (Rev 20:11; 21:1). 
 

The Final Judgement ‚Äď We believe in the Final Judgment of every person by the Son of Man, which will be done in Heaven (John 5:27; Rev. 20:11-15). That is to lay each person in their final destination according to their deeds while they were alive (Ro. 2:6-8; 2 Cor. 5:10; Matt 25:). There is an immediate judgment that God makes on a person who has died (Heb 9:27; Lk. 16:22-23), but God will do the Final Judgment to justify His verdict for their eternal destination (Matt. 7:21-23). 

 

The New Heaven and the New Earth ‚Äď We believe that God will put an absolute New Heaven and a New Earth (Rev. 21:1), after the Final Judgement and destruction of the Old Heaven and Earth (2 Pet. 3:10). The New Heaven and Earth will be the Eternal Kingdom of God, where He will put up the Holy City with His Throne in it (Rev.  21:2, 10). The Lord will be King over the victorious believers and His Holy Angels forever and ever, Amen (Rev. 11:15; 19:16).

Completion of all required subjects and units.

4.1 ATTENDANCE AND ACADEMIC RESPONSIBILITIES 

Regular attendance is required for all classes. 

Excessive absences may result in non-completion of a subject. 

Students are expected to complete all assignments, readings, and assessments. 

Students who have undergone advance lessons are exempted to online reporting provided they have presented a written report but are still required to attend online classes and take up final evaluation. 

4.2 STUDENT CONDUCT AND DISCIPLINE 

As a Christian training institution, students are expected to: 

Demonstrate respect toward instructors, classmates, and the learning environment. 

Show integrity in academic work and ministry activities. 

Maintain a teachable spirit and a commitment to spiritual growth. 

TBTI reserves the right to counsel, suspend, or dismiss students whose conduct is inconsistent with biblical values or school policies. 

4.3 ATTENDANCE POLICY 

The training program runs for 16 weeks per semester with regular class meetings designed to build consistency in study, spiritual growth, and discipline. 

Class Days: Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. 

Total Meetings: Three (3) sessions per week. 

Students are expected to attend all scheduled sessions unless prevented by valid and approved reasons. 

Punctuality and preparedness are considered part of Christian character and academic responsibility. 

Regular attendance is essential for spiritual formation and successful completion of the program. 

4.3.1 Punctuality 

Class begins promptly at 7:00 PM to 9:30 PM. 

7:15 PM onward is considered late. 

Students should arrive early to prepare spiritually and academically before class begins. 

Zoom link opens at 6:45 PM 

4.3.2 Late Policy 

Three (3) instances of lateness are equivalent to one (1) absence. 

Repeated lateness without valid excuses may affect participation grades and overall evaluation. 

4.3.3 Absences 

Students are expected to maintain consistent attendance throughout the semester. 

Frequent absences may result in academic consequences or disciplinary review. 

4.3.4 Excused Absences 

Absences will only be excused under valid circumstances such as: 

Medical illness or health-related concerns 

Family emergencies or urgent situations 

Students with excused absences must: 

Inform the instructor or administration promptly. 

Complete missed requirements, including watching the recorded lesson. 

Submit a written report summarizing the assigned video or missed lesson content. 

Unexcused absences may affect both academic standing and participation grades. 

4.4 LEARNING REQUIREMENTS 

4.4.1 Revelatory Notebook 

Each student must maintain a Revelatory Notebook to cultivate reflective learning and biblical insight. The notebook will be reviewed periodically. 

TBTI will provide a report sheet where students input their reading. 

4.4.2 Required Contents: 

Date of study or class session 

Number of hours spent studying or reading 

Specific Bible passages or reading ranges covered 

Personal reflections, key insights, and spiritual applications (encouraged) 

Failure to maintain an updated notebook may affect academic evaluation.
